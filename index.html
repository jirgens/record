CryFace

Slacker/DIY/Indie rock from Blissfield, Michigan. Encompassing styles from garage punk to AM soft rock and touching on everything in between. Started as a recording project in 2014, CryFace is now known for sweaty live shows and outsider humor in addition to diverse, technicolor albums.



Home George

Home George began in my living room when Brent Bryan picked up a cigar box ukulele I have. The uke is attractive but the harmonics are off, it having been built more as a novelty than a real instrument. That didn’t stop Brent who used a beer bottle as a slide, tuned the four strings into some chord or other, and proceeded to make music—real, hip swinging, toe tapping music.

I have played guitar and written songs for many years, mostly in my basement for nobody to hear. Brent encouraged me to visit his garage/recording studio and play him something. Before I got halfway through my first song, Brent suggested we record it, just for the heck of it. As is the case with collaboration, when it happens, the people involved don’t know it is happening. Brent brought real, hip swinging, toe tapping music to my rough outlines. He heard what I intended. Sometimes he heard the opposite. Mostly I was happy just to be heard and would do or try to do what Brent suggested. Play it fast. Play it slow. Play it in a different rhythm. Play it loud.

In this way, Home George is a collaboration of two men, neighbors on Maplewood Avenue, men who agree that making music is difficult work but shouldn’t sound that way.
